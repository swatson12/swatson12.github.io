Life Hack

Otago Polytechnic Bachelor of IT - Multimedia Development - Semester 2 2017

Planning

When I was planning the video, it took me a while to think of a topic for the video. I had thought about doing sometjing to do with the Raspberry Pi I was working on at the time, but it didn't seem very life hacky to me. I watched a number of life hack videos on Youtube for inspiration, so many so that I now can't escape the suggestions for life hack videos to watch. I decided to be cheeky about it and do a life hack everyone needs to do - drink water.

×










Putting it together

Due to bad time management, I didn't have a whole lot of time to learn how to use a software like Adobe Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve, so I used an app on my iPhone I'm familiar with, Splice. It was easy because I had filmed the clips on my phone, so all I had to do was export the completed file and upload it to a cloud drive so I acould access it on the computer.

Splice doesn't have a functionality to add text to the video, so I downloaded a free software called OpenShot Video Editor. It was easy to use and it didn't require a login or payment to export an HD video. With OpenShot, I added the credits and the last transition.
