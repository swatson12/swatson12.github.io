UI Kit

Otago Polytechnic Bachelor of IT - Multimedia Development - Semester 2 2017

×

For the UI Kit, I decided to base it off an app I'd already made that definitely could have the UI be developed a lot more. I spent a lot of time looking at other UI Kits on Google to get some inspiration to see how other apps and sites do their UI and what was sort of the latest style.



×

When we were first given the project, the first thing I did was make a list of all the UI features the app needs and uses. From there I made some sketches of what I thought the interactive parts could look like. Doing this helped the process so much because it gave me a way to get every initial idea I had for the kit out of my head and somewhere physical. It also made an excellent reference point if I ever needed to refer back if I forgot what parts I needed to add

When I was designing the UI kit, I spent a lot of time trying different styles and colours for the buttons. I used Photoshop a lot when designing the buttons, trying out different gradients and colours. I didn't end up using any of the buttons I made on Photoshop, sticking with a flat theme, but it was really interesting to make the buttons and see how they might have worked.

I decided to stick with the blue and yellow theme that Squash Otago uses, just to keep it consistent and to tie it back to Squash Otago. Using shades based directly off the Squash Otago blue and yellow made it easy to keep a theme happening and without using way too much bright yellow and blue. I used the colour picker on W3Schools to get shades and variants on the blue and yellow.
